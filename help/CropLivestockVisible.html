In this question we asking you to identify if there are crops or/and livestock in the photo you are looking at.

Livestock are an important asset that provide food, transport, and labour as work animal in agriculture. Their loss through disaster events can significantly affect livelihoods and have a flow on effect on the local economy.

Click if you see crop plants or agriculture fields, domestic animals or event both. Some of the crops you are likely to see in these photos includes rice, maize, wheat, barley, potato, and millet.

Some of the animals you are likely to see in these photos includes yak, donkeys, goats, sheep, and poultry.

You should also click if the crop or the animal appear dead as these can still give information about the environmental conditions in the area.

If you are not sure what you should look at for identify a crop, look for some of the following signs:
- Machinery, tools, irrigation, and other items associated with agriculture.
- Plants are in rows.
- Fences, hedgerows or other marking the boundaries of a field.
- Homogeneity in the field (all the plants are the same species, the same height, and/or look the same age).

Click if the photo shows a natural environment and it doesn’t show any form of livestock. Natural environments can be recognised by the following:

  • A variety of species growing in the same area.
  • Different layers in the vegetation that may include an understorey (such as grasses), a midstorey (smaller trees and shrubs), and an overstorey (the taller trees).
